Scrutinizing the Western Gaze: The ‘Turk’ in Eighteenth-Century European Art

Nebahat Avcıoğlu, Hunter College, City University of New York

A rich and complex historiographical tradition provides an opportunity to teach material through an innovative course structure.

Prompted by an invitation to write a bibliographic article for the Oxford Bibliographies Online, Nebahat Avcıoğlu designed and taught the innovative undergraduate research methods seminar "Scrutinizing the Western Gaze: The Turk in 18th Century European Art" [link to PDF] at Hunter College during Spring 2020. She was also interested in teaching the material as a category of interconnected and intercultural art that is rich in methodological issues, particularly with regard to the issues of trade, travel, diplomacy orientalism, and exoticism. Over the course of the semester, she and her students collectively compiled a digital bibliography on "Turquerie". She distributed the readings [link to PDF] between four groups of three students in order to cover the extensive bibliography she had previously compiled. In place of a final research paper, each student presented five readings and drafted annotations, while working together with their group to produce a final version.

Nebahat Avcıoğlu is an associate professor at Hunter College, City University of New York. A specialist in Islamic architecture and art with a particular emphasis on Ottoman European cultural encounters, she co-edited with Finbarr Barry Flood a watershed special issue of Ars Orientalis, "Globalizing Cultures: Art and Mobility in the 18th century."
